West Campus Roadway Connection Analysis




                               Introduction
                               Roadway connections between the Main Campus, Ox Road (Route 123) and
                               Braddock Road through the West Campus are concepts developed through the
                               George Mason University Fairfax Campus Transportation Plan. This plan was
                               developed through a collaboration process involving University, City, County,
                               and State representatives. These connections provide a series of local, regional,
                               and campus benefits and are a key opportunity for partnership between project
                               stakeholders including Fairfax City, Fairfax County, VDOT, and George Mason
                               University. There are several components to these connections as outlined below:
                               •	Improvements to University Drive west of Route 123 (Ox Road).
                               •	Construction of a new road following Rapidan River Road from University Drive
                                 to Braddock Road near the Prestwick Drive/West Campus Parking Lot entrance.
                               •	Construction of a new road to the south of the field house
                                 including a grade-separated crossing of Route 123 to
                                 Mason Pond Drive near the Mason Inn access road.
                               This memorandum presents the results of an analysis of the West Campus connections
                               completed for George Mason University in conjunction with the Parking and Transportation
                               Master Plan. Three different scenarios for these connections are analyzed in this report:
                               •	University Connect: Connection only via University Drive.
                               •	Overpass: Connection only via the grade-separated crossing.
                               •	Both Connections: Connection through both routes.



                               Roadway Concepts
                               The improvements to University Drive are proposed to include modifications to the
                               alignment, cross-section, and roadway connections. University Drive would be shifted to
                               the south to improve the buffer between the street and the existing residential properties
                               to the north. Improvements to the cross-section would include installation of planted
                               buffers between the sidewalks/bike paths and the street, installation of a landscaped
                               median, and development of a consistent four-lane cross-section with turning lanes where
                               appropriate. On-street parking would be removed as part of this project. The roadway
                               would be aligned to connect directly to the Braddock Road connection, with Fairfax
                               Villa connecting as a T-intersection to the west end of University Drive. Stop signs and
                               pavement markings would be installed on all side street approaches, and the traffic signal
                               at Route 123 and University Drive would be modified to accommodate the connection.

                               The realignment of University Drive would continue as a new roadway connection,
                               roughly following the Rapidan River Road alignment, and would connect with
                               Braddock Road opposite Prestwick Drive, at the existing access point to the West
                               Campus Parking Lot. Connections to the athletic fields and associated parking lots
                               would be reconfigured as part of this plan. The West Campus parking lot would
                               also be reconfigured to accommodate a new road. Additionally, a new traffic
                               signal would be installed on Braddock Road where this new roadway connects.
                               Sidewalks and bike paths would also be installed along this connection.

                               Figure 1 shows approximate alignments of these roadway connections. If these roadways
                               were to be built, the alignment, including connections to existing streets and connections
                               to parking areas, would need to be developed and refined during the design process.

                          Roadway Benefits
                          The new connections would provide benefits in terms of campus access,
                          neighborhood accessibility, and regional traffic flow.
                          •	Campus Access: traffic destined to and from the George Mason University Main
                            Campus could use the University Drive and southern grade-separated connections
                            to access the campus. As a result, traffic volumes on Braddock Road at Route 123,
                            Roanoke River Road, and Nottoway River Road will be reduced. These connections
                            will also help reduce event traffic impacts during arrival and departure periods.
                          •	Neighborhood Connections: the Fairfax Villa and Chancery Park neighborhoods
                            would be afforded with a new connection to Braddock Road west, without
                            needing to travel through the Braddock Road/Route 123 intersection. On-
                            street parking on University Drive would be eliminated and access to the Fairfax
                            Villa neighborhood would be more sheltered from University traffic.
                          •	Regional Traffic Flow: providing the west campus connection would reduce regional
                            traffic congestion on Braddock Road and Route 123 by reducing the amount of eastbound
                            left-turn traffic from Braddock Road to Route 123 and the southbound right-turn traffic
                            from Route 123 to Braddock Road. These movements have the option of using the new
                            University Drive/Rapidan River Road link to by-pass this intersection. In addition, campus-
                            related traffic is reduced at the Braddock Road/Route 123 intersection as described above.



                          Analysis Scenarios
                          Five separate analyses were completed. Existing conditions were first analyzed. Then, future
                          conditions for a design year of 2020 were completed, with no west campus connection
                          assumed. Assumptions about development on George Mason University property and
                          surrounding the campus were made, and Braddock Road and Route 123 interim improvements
                          were also assumed. Finally, future conditions were analyzed with three variations of
                          the west campus connection in place. The intersections that were analyzed are:
                          1.	Braddock Road @ Prestwick Drive/West Campus Connector
                          2.	Braddock Road @ Ox Road (Route 123)
                          3.	Braddock Road @ Roanoke River Road/University Mall Entrance
                          4.	Braddock Road @ Nottoway River Lane/Sideburn Road
                          5.	Braddock Road @ Roberts Road
                          6.	Ox Road (Route 123) @ University Drive
                          7.	University Drive @ George Mason Boulevard
                          Comparisons between the future scenarios were made to determine
                          how much the proposed connection would improve traffic flow through
                          the area. The analyzed intersections are shown on Figure 1.



                          Existing Conditions
                          Existing traffic counts were collected in April 2010 at the study intersections. Traffic volumes were
                          analyzed using Synchro, Version 7. Synchro files for the traffic signals were obtained from the
                          Virginia Department of Transportation, and these files use the existing traffic signal phasings,
                          timings, and offsets that are currently in the field. All of the signals are in Fairfax County and are
                          controlled by VDOT. Figure 2 illustrates the existing traffic volumes at each of the study intersections
                          along with the existing lane configurations at each intersection. Table 1 lists the current levels
                          of service at each intersection, by approach and overall, along with delays in seconds.

                          Future Conditions (2020) Without West Campus Connection
                          For the future conditions both with and without a West Campus roadway connection, both
                          projected development in the area and ambient traffic growth was added to the existing
                          volumes. First, a two percent growth rate for existing traffic was assumed based on historic
                          growth trends along both Braddock Road and Route 123 and compounded each year from
                          2010 to 2020. Second, projected growth at George Mason University was calculated and
                          assigned to the roadway network. The trip generation for University-related growth is shown
                          below in Table 2, and includes an enrollment increase of 5,091 students and the 148-room
                          Mason Inn Hotel and Conference Center, which was not open at the time of data collection.

                           Use                 AM In   AM Out    AM Total   PM In    PM Out    PM Total   ADT

                          In addition to expansion on the campus, there is also a planned redevelopment
                          of University Mall. The additional site traffic from this expansion was detailed in
                          an analysis completed in September 2009, and the site traffic from this report was
                          added directly to the traffic network. Site traffic assignments with the closure of the
                          median break access to University Mall between Route 123 and Roanoke River Road
                          were utilized to be consistent with future roadway configuration assumptions.

                          Also assumed for the future conditions are planned interim improvements along Braddock
                          Road and Route 123 and at the intersection of these two major roadways. An interchange
                          is planned to be eventually constructed at this location; however, no funding is available,
                          so this project could not be assumed for 2020. As a part of the interchange design concept,
                          interim lane improvements are planned and should be completed by the 2020 design
                          year. These improvements include additional through lanes along Route 123 near the
                          intersection in both directions and additional left-turn lanes along both the northbound
                          and southbound approach of Route 123 at Braddock Road. Each approach at Braddock
                          and Route 123 will have dual left-turn lanes, three through lanes, and a right-turn lane.

                          Along Braddock Road, three through lanes in each direction were assumed past the intersection
                          with Route 123 and with Roanoke River Road, with these three lanes tapering back to the
                          existing two lanes in each direction between Roanoke River Road and Nottoway River Lane.
                          The left-turn lane into Roanoke River Road along eastbound Braddock Road is also planned to
                          be lengthened, as the median crossover to the west of it will be closed, and dual westbound
                          left-turns lanes into University Mall are also planned. At the intersection of Braddock Road
                          and Roanoke River Road, an additional northbound lane, and an additional southbound lane
                          are assumed, allowing for a separate left-turn lane, a separate through lane, and a separate
                          right-turn lane along each of these approaches. This would also alter the signal phasing.
                          Existing conditions include split-phasing, with the northbound and southbound movements
                          operating under separate phases. Under future conditions, with the separate left-turn lanes, it
                          was assumed that the left-turns would have a concurrent protected phase, then the through
                          movements and right-turn movement would move concurrently, with left-turns permitted.
                          This would allow for more efficient signal operations than under existing conditions.

                          Future Conditions (2020) With West Campus Connection
                          For the future conditions with the west campus connection, the same assumptions were
                          made for ambient traffic growth, George Mason University expansion, and University Mall
                          expansion. As noted previously, new roadway connections through west campus should
                          redistribute some traffic, especially University-related traffic from the west, from the heavily
                          congested intersection of Braddock Road and Route 123 and redirect some University traffic
                          away from the congested entrance at Roanoke River Road as well as from Nottoway River Lane.

                          University Drive Connection Only
                          If the west campus connection were to be constructed generally along University Drive and
                          Rapidan River Road, a portion of the existing traffic would divert to the roadway, including traffic
                          headed into George Mason University from the west and regional traffic. It was assumed that 30
                          percent of the traffic using the Roanoke River Road and Nottoway River Lane entrances to campus
                          from Braddock Road west of Route 123 would use the new roadway. Based on the existing
                          volumes at the Braddock Road/Route 123 intersection, 90 percent of all eastbound left turns at
                          these intersections were assumed to come from points west on Braddock Road. Additionally, it
                          was assumed that 70 percent of GMU traffic from west of Route 123 turning right into University
                          Drive would be diverted to the connection. Existing volumes suggest that 30 percent of all
                          northbound right turns from Route 123 at University Drive come from the west on Braddock Road.

                           Some traffic traveling between areas west along Braddock Road and the City of Fairfax was
                          diverted to the new road, as it would provide another, possibly faster option, to connect from
                          Braddock Road to University Drive and Route 123 and vice versa. The Metropolitan Washington
                          Council of Governments (COG) long-range regional travel demand forecasting model was run
                          with and without the University Drive west campus connection to identify potential users of the
                          roadway. The model assigns traffic between origins and destinations throughout the region to
                          significant roadways based on relative travel times. Thus, traffic would be removed from Braddock
                          Road and Route 123 and diverted to the University Drive connection, if the total travel time to the
                          destination was shorter. The model assigned approximately 4,800 vehicles per day to the new
                          roadway link, in addition to those trips to and from land uses abutting the connection. Given the
                          prevailing hourly and directional distribution on the roadways in the vicinity, this would equate
                          to approximately 270 peak hour, peak direction trips and 115 peak hour, off-peak direction trips.

                           Grade-Separated Connection Only

                          If a west campus connection were constructed with a bridge over Route 123 south of the Field
                          House, this would provide a more direct connection to areas between the Mason Inn and Patriot

                          right turns from Route 123 to University Drive were diverted to the new connection.

                          If both the University Drive and grade-separated connections through west campus were constructed,

                          University Drive from Braddock Road west of Route 123 were redistributed through west campus.

West Campus Roadway Connection Analysis




                          Braddock Road/Ox Road (Route 123)
                          The construction of a connection between Braddock Road and Route 123 would divert traffic
                          away from this intersection. Peak hour levels of service would be improved from overall LOS
                          ‘F’ to overall LOS ‘E’ conditions with any of the west campus connection scenarios. While
                          all approaches would continue to operate below LOS ‘D’, most would be at LOS ‘E’ with
                          any of the new roadways, rather than LOS ‘F’ with no connection. Overall delays would be
                          reduced from 5 to 15 percent, with higher reductions in delay on some approaches.

                          Braddock Road/Roanoke River Road/University Mall Entrance
                          Due to fewer vehicles from the west accessing George Mason University via this location, the
                          Braddock Road/Roanoke River Road/University Mall Entrance intersection would experience
                          improvements in delays and levels of service during the PM peak hour, with a west campus
                          connection. Overall LOS ‘D’ or better conditions, with some LOS ‘E’ approaches, would
                          continue during the AM peak hour with any connection scenario. During the PM peak
                          hour, the overall intersection level of service would be improved from LOS ‘F’ to LOS ‘E’, and
                          delays reduced by 43 to 51 percent, with any of the new roadways. Individual intersection
                          approaches would be improved from mostly LOS ‘F’ to mostly LOS ‘E’ conditions.

                          Braddock Road/Nottoway River Lane/Sideburn Road
                          Relatively minor improvements in level of service and delay at the Braddock Road/
                          Nottoway River Lane/Sideburn Road intersection result from any of the west campus
                          connections. The AM peak hour overall LOS ‘D’ without a connection is improved
                          to an overall LOS ‘C’ with any of the new roadways, while all future scenarios result
                          in overall LOS ‘C’ during the PM peak hour. Overall intersection delays of four to
                          eight percent at this location would result from a west campus connection.

                          Braddock Road/Roberts Road
                          Only minor changes in approach and overall intersection delays would be realized at
                          the Braddock Road/Roberts Road intersection with a west campus connection. Overall
                          intersection delays would change by -0.3 to +1.3 percent, with some minor increases
                          in delay on some approaches. The University Drive connection option during the AM
                          peak hour is the only scenario with a reduction in overall intersection delay.

                          Ox Road (Route 123)/University Drive
                          The west campus connections result in a redistribution of traffic at the Route 123/University Drive
                          intersection, particularly with direct connections at this point. Thus, some approaches experience
                          increases in traffic, and some decreases in traffic, for the various scenarios. The AM peak hour level
                          of service remains at ‘D’ for all future scenarios, with overall intersection delays reduced by four
                          to nine percent. During the PM peak hour, the overall intersection operation is improved from
                          LOS ‘F’ to LOS ‘E’ or better with any of the west campus connections, with reductions in overall
                          delay of 36 to 55 percent. These equate to reductions in vehicular delays of 44 to 67 seconds per
                          vehicle. Individual intersection approach delays would experience slight increases or substantial
                          decreases in delays. It is noted that a portion of the operational improvement at this location is
                          due to the additional lanes on University Drive associated with the cross-section enhancements.

                          University Drive/George Mason Boulevard
                          The University Drive/George Mason Boulevard intersection would operate at
                          desirable overall LOS’C’ conditions under all future scenarios. Modest increases
                          or decreases in approach delay would result with the various west campus
                          roadway configurations. Overall intersection delays would change by -1.9 to +20.2
                          percent, equating to changes in vehicular delay of -0.5 to +5.6 seconds.

                          Braddock Road/West Campus Connector/Prestwick Drive
                          The intersection of the new west campus roadway and Braddock Road was also analyzed.
                          This intersection would require signalization, and it was assumed that it would run with half
                          the cycle length as the signal at Braddock Road and Route 123 and be included along the
                          Braddock Road signal corridor. There would be a single eastbound left-turn into the new
                          roadway as well as a single westbound right-turn lane. There is currently a westbound left-
                          turn lane along Braddock Road onto Prestwick Drive, though there is no eastbound right-turn
                          lane. The Braddock Road/Route 123 interim improvements include additional through lanes
                          along Braddock, which would extend to the location of this intersection, so three through
                          lanes along Braddock Road were assumed. No eastbound right-turn lane onto Prestwick
                          Drive was assumed with these improvements. Along northbound Prestwick Drive, there is
                          a single exiting lane, which was assumed for the future, and for the southbound movement,
                          a shared left-turn/through lane was assumed as well as two separate right-turn lanes.

                          Under any of the scenarios with a west campus connection, this signalized intersection would
                          operate at desirable overall LOS ‘D’ or better conditions, with some individual LOS ‘E’ approaches.



                          Conclusions
                          A new roadway connection though west campus, if constructed between Braddock
                          Road and Route 123, would provide benefits to campus access, enhance neighborhood
                          connections, and improve regional traffic flow. Diversion of University-related traffic from
                          Braddock Road and its intersections with Roanoke River Road and Nottoway River Lane
                          would reduce delays considerably for some turning movements to and from campus.

                          Residents of Fairfax Villa and Chancery Park would gain a more direct connection
                          to Braddock Road to and from the west, without traversing the congested
                          intersections of Route 123 with University Drive and Braddock Road. Delays
                          entering and exiting the new roadway from Braddock Road would be significantly
                          shorter than conditions at the intersection of Braddock Road and Route 123.

                          The increase in roadway capacity serving University and regional traffic would
                          improve the operation of the Braddock Road/Route 123 intersection, with overall
                          improvements of 5 to 15 percent for the various configurations. In addition to the
                          benefits for campus traffic at the Braddock Road/Roanoke River Road intersection,
                          delays for regional through traffic would also be reduced at this location.
