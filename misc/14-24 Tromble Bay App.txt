                                        NARRATIVE

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

The subject 39.62 acre + parcel is currently owned by Tromble Land Company, LLC and the
contract purchaser of the property is GBU Ventures, LLC. The subject property is currently
has two single family homes and several horse stables and is located on the southeast
corner of Pine Street and Morningside Road Englewood, Florida. The subject parcel is
within the Pine Street, Dearborn Street, and South River Road Sector Plan (No, 89-01-SP).
The property’s currently future land use designation is Low Density Residential on the
Sarasota County Future Land Use Map. This proposed Rezone Petition is a request to
rezone the 39.62 acre + parcel from OUE-1 (Open Use Estate, 1 dwelling unit per 5 acres)
to RSF-1 (Residential Single Family, 2.5 dwelling units per acre) consistent with the intent of
the designation of Low Density Residential on the Sarasota County Comprehensive Plan
Future Land Use Map.

The project being called Tromble Bay is a proposed single family clustered subdivision of 79
single family homes in a price range of approximately $350,000. The proposed clustered
subdivision with a total of 79 single family homes is intended to be compatible with the
adjacent single family homes in the Stillwater to the south. A 20 foot landscape buffer and
fence is shown on the non binding Development Concept Plan along the northern perimeter
of the property along Morningside Drive to avoid disruption of existing homeowners on the
north side of Morningside Dr. Enhanced landscaping will be used along the northern buffer
with a fence to ensure the isolation of the proposed community at the request of the
adjacent homeowners


PINE STREET, DEARBORN STREET, AND SOUTH RIVER ROAD SECTOR PLAN (NO.
89-01-SP),

The subject parcel is included as a study area within the Pine Street, Dearborn Street, and
South River Road Sector Plan (No. 89-01-SP), Ordinance No. 93-059. It is not shown as a
subject parcel within a Planning Area that reflects density but it is directly adjacent to
Planning Area A which is a mixed use parcel.

The subject parcel is within both study areas 2B and 3. These areas are not within the
detailed planning areas but must comply with the Urban Design techniques of the Sector
Plan. The Plan assists in formulating desirable land use distributions and allocations
consistent with an analysis of the areas’ constraints and opportunities and provides
mechanisms to influence the timing of future development and redevelopment on an
areawide basis.       This planning approach is appropriate because these areawide
assessments provide a mechanism to assess impacts on a cumulative basis rather than a
site specific, fragmented basis.

Requested Clustered Subdivision and Intent of the RSF District

According to Section 4.6.2.of the Zoning Ordinance, Residential Single-Family District (RSF)
provides for development to occur as a cluster subdivision. Specifically:

               a. The RSF Districts permit single-family residential densities, which may take
                the form of single-family detached or cluster subdivisions. Cluster subdivisions
                may include lot line, traditional, patio, villa, atrium, duplex, or townhouse
                units. The nature of the use of property is the same in all RSF Districts, with
                variation among the RSF-1, RSF-2, RSF-3 and RSF-4 Districts in the
                requirements for density, lot area and width, and yards. A limited number of
                nonresidential uses are allowed, subject to the restrictions and requirements
                necessary to preserve and protect the single-family residential character of the
                neighborhood.

               b. The RSF Districts are generally used to implement the Comprehensive Plan
               within those areas shown as Low Density, Moderate Density and Medium
               Density Residential on the Future Land Use Map and on the Barrier Islands
               where the application of the district would not increase the density of
               development or hamper hurricane evacuation planning and disaster mitigation
               efforts. Locations on the Barrier Islands that have existing RSF zoning are
               acknowledged, and are considered to be consistent with the Future Land Use
               Map. The RSF-4 District is also used to implement the Comprehensive Plan in
               the areas shown as Office/Multifamily Residential Area and Corridor on the
               Future Land Use. The RSF-4 District, while not a primary implementing district,
               may also be allowed in Regional, Community, Village I, Village II, and
               Neighborhood Commercial Centers. Appropriate densities within the Low.
               Moderate or Medium Density Residential areas shall be guided by the density
               ranges as outlined in Future Land Use Policy 3.1.1 of the Comprehensive Plan.
               These densities shall also be determined, in part, by the land use designations
               and developed residential areas surrounding the parcel.

A rezoning of this property from OUE-1 to RSF-1 is consistent with the Sarasota County
Future Land Use Plan. NOTE: A rezoning to the RSF-1 does not require a binding
Development Concept Plan.

Environment

The subject parcel has been inspected by staff of WRA an engineering firm specializing in
Civil Engineering, Water Resource Planning and Environmental Analysis. WRA
Environmental Scientist classified habitat types based on fieldwork consisting of traversing
the entire site by foot and review of current Soil Survey of Sarasota County on GIS. In
addition, preliminary listed species observations were performed on the site on separate
occasions to identify the presence of any endangered, threatened and species of special
concern. These surveys comprised of pedestrian transects throughout the majority of the
site.

    •   the uplands consist of a mixture of isolated and connected native habitat
    •   the site does have wetlands or other surface waters
    •   No observations of endangered or threatened species were noted to be present


The proposed configuration of open space was designed with connectivity to other open use
areas as a priority. With existing off-site habitats to the south, placing a large amount of the
open space to the south side of the project area would create the best solution for buffering
habitats. By doing so it creates linkage for wildlife utilizing the upland areas for movement
and foraging opportunities. The proposed design is consistent with management guidelines
and the policies found in the Sarasota Comprehensive Plan and provides for greater
environmental functions and value for long term connectivity.            The detailed report is
submitted as Attachment D.


Potable Water/Wastewater Treatment

The subject parcel is within the franchise area of the Englewood Water District for the
provision of both water service and sewer service. There are services adjacent to the site for
tie-in. Detailed tie-in locations will be determined during the S&D/County Utility permitting
process.

There are adequate capacities in the Englewood Water District utilities to serve the
proposed development with both potable water and sanitary sewer service. Based upon the
forgoing, the proposed Rezone Petition will be consistent with Objective 2.2, 2.3 and Policy
2.2.1 of the Future Land Use Chapter of the Comprehensive Plan which requires adequate
public facilities be available at the time of development. As such, the Application can be
found to be consistent with applicable goals, objectives and policies the Comprehensive
Plan.

Stormwater/Drainage

The subject parcel is located within two distinct County drainage basins: Approximately
54.4% of the site is located in the Gottfried Creek Drainage Basin and the remaining 45.6%
is located in the Ainger Creek Drainage Basin.

Transportation

The project proposes to make connection to Pine Street, a 2-lane collector with a posted
speed limit of 45 mph and an secondary access onto Morningside Drive as far west as
possible to avoid complications with the existing homeowners down Morningside Drive.

A formal traffic study, prepared by RAYSOR Transportation Consulting, is included with the
submittal to address any transportation concerns for the project as Attachment E.
According to the traffic study the intersections and roadway segments were found to
currently operate acceptably and it is anticipated that they will continue to operate
acceptably for future background traffic.

Based upon reviews by Transportation Staff and stipulations proffered by the Applicant, the
application can be found to be consistent with applicable goals, objectives and policies the
Comprehensive Plan.

Land Use /Zoning

The subject parcel currently has two single family residences and horse stables. It is zoned
OUE-1 (Residential Estate, one dwelling unit per 5 acres). The subject parcel is located
adjacent to Stillwater Subdivision to the south, which is zoned RSF-1. Property’s to the
north are zoned OUE-1 with the future land use designation of low density residential.

The proposed rezoning request is to RSF-1 (Residential Single Family, 2.5 dwelling units
per acre), a zone district consistent with the adopted Sector Plan and the Sarasota County
Comprehensive Plan. A total project density of 1.98 dwelling units per acre is requested for
79 total units, which includes 30 percent open space and a focal point which includes a
access to natural habitat areas.

Project Values

Construction costs for the entire project including site work are estimated at a little over $23
million. The sale prices of individual home sites in Tromble Bay are estimated between
$325,000 and $400,000. Based upon a total of the 79 homes, the estimated value of the
homes is between $25,675,000 and $31,600,000. No affordable housing units are
anticipated and the number of school aged children in all units is estimated at less than 40
children.

Compatibility

Pine Street is an existing two lane collector roadway from the intersection of S. River Rd to
the north and S. McCall Rd to the south which crosses into Charlotte County. A proposed
10 foot landscaped roadway buffer is proposed along the project frontage on Pine Street
and the northern and southern portions of the subject parcel propose a 20 foot landscaped
buffer. An fully opaque fence is also proposed along the entire north property boundary to
help buffer the existing residents along the north side of Morningside Drive. Along the
eastern boundary is an open space preservation area and therefore no open space buffers
area required. This area is currently vegetated with Pine Flatwoods that will be preserved
and supplemented with native trees to serve to create a shaded buffer for pedestrians and
residents using the focal point area of the project.

The proposed 79 lot clustered subdivision was discussed with nearby residents at the
Neighborhood Workshop held on July 9, 2014. Concerns were raised mainly by the
adjacent homeowners along the north side of Morningside Drive and how this effect their
rural neighborhood. The applicant has agreed to provide a minimum 20’ well landscaped
buffer with an opaque fence/wall to avoid disruption of the existing homeowners. Also the
main access has been coordinated in depth with Sarasota County in order to allow for the
main access to connect to Pine Street instead of Morningside Drive. This will help alleviate
the concerns raised regarding the added traffic to their local roadway. The proposed single
story homes are intended to be compatible with the adjacent single story single family
homes to the south within the Stillwater Community. The proposed Non Binding
Development Concept Plan will bring the parcel into conformity with the vision of the Sector
Plan and support the compatibility of the adjacent development patterns.

The Applicant’s desire is to help implement the vision of the adopted Sector Plan. The Non
Binding Development Concept Plan in Attachment B illustrates, and the Applicant will
stipulate to all conditions already contained in the adopted in the Sector Plan including
urban design considerations including signage, lighting, and vegetation.

Proffered Stipulations

    As part of this Rezone Application, the Applicant proffers the following stipulations:

    1. Development of the subject parcel shall be consistent with all applicable conditions of
       the adopted Pine Street, Dearborn Street, and South River Road Sector Plan.

    2. The Master Surface Water Management Plan shall be consistent with the Ainger
       Creek and Gottfried Creek Basin Master plans.

    3. All stormwater treatment shall be open and above ground.

    4. Pedestrian access from all residences on the subject property shall be provided
       along Pine Street and in a location near the center of the property heading south,
       with the purpose of providing a pedestrian access to the Medical Blvd activity center.

    5. There shall be a minimum 20 foot wide, 0.7 opacity landscape buffer provided along
       the northern boundary and a 10 foot street buffer along Pine Street.

    6. The maximum height of all structures shall not exceed two stories in height.

    7. The site will be landscaped with Florida Friendly Landscape materials. The retention
       ponds shall be planted with a vegetated shoreline buffer.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the proposed project is consistent with the Sarasota County Comprehensive
Plan and the adopted Pine Street, Dearborn Street, and South River Road Sector Plan (No.
89-01-SP), Ordinance No. 93-059. Furthermore, the rezoning is consistent based upon the
location of this parcel near an OPI Office and Professional center and adjacent RSF-1
residential community. The parcel size is adequate to accommodate the density of 79 single
family homes as a unified development. Given the safeguards already contained in the
adopted Sector Plan, this project will be compatible with the surrounding area.
