DESCR IPT ION OF DEVELOPMENT
StonebridgeCarras is planning to pursue a redevelopment of the existing uses in the Oakville
Triangle, located west of US 1 in Alexandria, bordered by Calvert Avenue to the north, Fannon Street
to the south, and the Mt. Jefferson Trail to the west. The development project will replace a mix of
highway-oriented uses with a mixture of housing, retail, and hotel development in a walkable, urban
form.

As a part of the redevelopment process, the City of Alexandria has requested that StonebridgeCarras
participate in a small-small area planning process as well as the normal Development Special Use
Permit (DSUP) process associated with the development of land in the city. The study area is shown
in Figure 1.

ANALY SIS MET HODOLOGY
The study intersections will be analyzed using Highway Capacity Manual (HCM) Methodologies and
the Synchro Software Package. Analysis will be performed for existing conditions, Phase 1
conditions, Phase 2 Full Build-Out conditions, and Phase 2 Full Build-Out plus 6 years. Analysis will
consider the AM and PM commuter peak hours of travel.

VISSIM modeling of the study network will be as specified by the City of Alexandria Transportation
Study Guidelines.

INT ERNAL C APT URE
Internal capture between land uses is assumed to be represented by the pedestrian and bicycle
mode split reductions. No additional Internal Capture will be calculated. This is in accordance with the
methodologies used in the December 2009 Potomac Yard Multimodal Transportation Study.

PASS-BY
As the planned on-site retail is not large format, no pass-by trips reductions will be assumed for the
on-site retail uses in accordance with the methodologies used in the December 2009 Potomac Yard
Multimodal Transportation Study. A pass-by trip percentage of 43% of PM trips will be assumed for
high-turnover (sit-down) restaurant uses in accordance with the methodologies of the ITE Trip
Generation Manual.
