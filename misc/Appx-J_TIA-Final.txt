I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report presents the results of the transportation impact analysis (TIA) for the proposed Fair Oaks
Bridge Rehabilitation, located in the City of Sunnyvale, California. The project is located on Fair Oaks
Avenue and is generally bounded by Kifer Avenue to the north and East Evelyn Avenue to the south. The
project will rehabilitate the existing Fair Oaks Bridge spanning the Caltrain tracks and East Hendy Avenue.
The rehabilitated bridge will retain four travel lanes for cars, 6-foot wide bicycle lanes in both directions
and a 6-foot wide sidewalk along the east side of the bridge. This analysis includes an evaluation of traffic
conditions during and after construction at two intersections.



TRANSIT SERVICE

The proposed project study area includes four existing Santa Clara VTA bus routes. Within the study area,
VTA bus route 26 and 304 currently operate on Fair Oaks Avenue, VTA bus route 32 operates on Evelyn
Avenue and VTA bus route 55 operates on Sunnyvale Avenue.


BICYCLE AND PEDESTRIAN FACILITIES

The project will allow for the addition of a standard sized sidewalk and wider-than-existing bicycle lanes.
Near the project site, there are existing class II bicycle lanes in both directions on Kifer Road, Evelyn
Avenue, Sunnyvale Avenue and Wolfe Rd.

A sidewalk will be provided on the east side of the Fair Oaks Avenue Bridge. The sidewalk will connect
with those currently provided on Kifer Road, the east side of Fair Oaks Avenue north of the Fair Oaks
Bridge, the east side of Fair Oaks Avenue south of the Fair Oaks Bridge.

To facilitate pedestrian access to the Fair Oaks Bridge, the existing crosswalks will be enhanced. On the
south end of the bridge at the Fair Oaks Avenue/Evelyn Avenue intersection, curb ramps parallel to
crosswalks will be added at the northeast, southeast and southwest corners. The Fair Oaks Avenue/Evelyn
Avenue intersection will also include four signal controlled crosswalks. On the north end of the bridge at
the Kifer Road /Fair Oaks Avenue intersection, curb ramps parallel to crosswalks will be added on the
northeast, northwest and southeast corners. The Fair Oaks/Kifer Avenue intersection will include
crosswalks on the north leg and east leg of the intersection.

Since at least one lane in each direction will be maintained on Fair Oaks during the bridge rehabilitation
the construction activities will not have significant impacts to traffic. We do expect some traffic to avoid
the construction area and route to Wolfe Road and Sunnyvale Avenue via Evelyn Avenue and California

1. INTRODUCTION

This report presents the results of the transportation impact analysis (TIA) for the Fair Oaks Bridge
Rehabilitation located in the City of Sunnyvale, California. The project will rehabilitate the existing Fair
Oaks Bridge spanning the Caltrain tracks and East Hendy Avenue extending from Kifer Road to Evelyn
Avenue. The site location is shown on the map on Figure 1.

The purpose of this analysis is to identify potentially significant adverse impacts during the rehabilitation
construction period or impacts associated with proposed intersection lane configuration changes on the
surrounding transportation system. The TIA was prepared following the guidelines of the City of
Sunnyvale and Santa Clara Valley Transportation Authority (VTA), the congestion management agency for
Santa Clara County.


PROJECT DESCRIPTION

    •    As proposed, the project would rehabilitate the Fair Oaks Bridge spanning the Caltrain tracks and
         East Hendy Avenue. The rehabilitated bridge will retain four travel lanes for cars, a 6-foot wide
         sidewalk on the east side of the bridge and 6-foot wide bicycle lanes. The following minor lane
         configuration changes will be incorporated at the Fair Oaks Avenue/Evelyn Avenue and Fair Oaks
         Avenue/Kiefer Road intersections:
    •    Fair Oaks Avenue/Evelyn Avenue
              o Northbound: The existing right-turn slip lane will be removed to improve pedestrian
                 conditions.
              o Southbound: A single left-turn lane will replace the existing dual left-turn lanes.
              o Westbound: The existing right-turn slip lane will be removed to improve pedestrian
                 conditions.
    •    Fair Oaks Avenue/Kifer Road
              o Northbound: The existing right-turn slip lane will be removed to improve pedestrian
                 conditions.
              o Westbound: The existing right-turn slip lane will be removed to improve pedestrian
                 conditions.

To facilitate pedestrian access to the Fair Oaks Bridge, the existing crosswalks will be enhanced. On the
south end of the bridge at the Fair Oaks Avenue/Evelyn Avenue intersection, curb ramps parallel to
crosswalks will be added at the northeast, southeast and southwest corners. The Fair Oaks Avenue/Evelyn
Avenue intersection will also include four signal controlled crosswalks. On the north end of the bridge at
the Kifer Road /Fair Oaks Avenue intersection, curb ramps parallel to crosswalks will be added on the
northeast, northwest and southeast corners. The Fair Oaks/Kifer Avenue intersection will include
crosswalks on the north leg and east leg of the intersection.

ANALYSIS METHODS

The operations of roadway facilities are described with the term level of service. Level of Service (LOS) is a
qualitative description of traffic flow based on such factors as speed, travel time, delay, and freedom to
maneuver. Six levels are defined from LOS A, as the best operating conditions, to LOS F, or the worst
operating conditions. LOS E represents “at-capacity” operations. When traffic volumes exceed the
intersection capacity, stop-and-go conditions result, and operations are designated as LOS F.

SIGNALIZED INTERSECTIONS

The method described in Chapter 16 of the 2000 Highway Capacity Manual (HCM) (Special Report 209,
Transportation Research Board) was used to prepare the level of service calculations for the study
intersections. This level of service method, which is approved by the City of Sunnyvale and VTA, analyzes a
signalized intersection’s operation based on average control delay per vehicle. Control delay includes the
initial deceleration delay, queue move-up time, stopped delay, and final acceleration delay. The average
control delay for signalized intersections is calculated using TRAFFIX analysis software and is correlated to
a LOS designation as shown in Table 1.

The City of Sunnyvale’s minimum threshold for acceptable signalized intersection operations is LOS D for
the study intersection.

2. EXISTING CONDITIONS

This chapter describes the existing conditions of the roadway facilities, pedestrian and bicycle facilities,
and transit service. It also presents existing traffic volumes and operations for the study intersections and
freeway segments with the results of level of service calculations.


EXISTING ROADWAY NETWORK

The following streets are within close proximity to the bridge rehabilitation project:

Fair Oaks Avenue is a four to five lane north-south roadway that extends through Sunnyvale from SR 237
in the north and continuing as West Remington Drive in the south. It connects El Camino Real, Central
Expressway, the Bayshore Freeway and the Southbay Freeway. Fair Oaks Avenue travels in a north-south
direction.

California Avenue is a four-lane, east-west roadway that extends from North Mary Avenue to merge with
the eastbound Central Expressway at Fair Oaks Avenue.

Evelyn Avenue is a four-five lane, east-west roadway that extends between Castro Street in the City of
Mountain View and Reed Avenue in the City of Sunnyvale. Evelyn Avenue provides access to both the
Sunnyvale and Mountain View Caltrain stations.

Kifer Avenue is a four to five-lane, east-west roadway that extends from Fair Oaks Avenue to Bowers
Avenue. This roadway runs parallel to the Central Expressway and continues as Walsh Avenue to the west.

East Hendy Ave is a two-lane, east-west roadway that extends from North Taffe Street to Kifer Road. It
runs parallel to the Caltrain tracks.


PEDESTRIAN FACILITIES

Pedestrian facilities consist of sidewalks, crosswalks, and pedestrian signals at signalized intersections. In
the vicinity of the project site, sidewalks are currently provided on both sides of Kifer Road, the east side
of North Fair Oaks Avenue, the east and west side of South Fair Oaks Avenue and the north and south
side of Evelyn Avenue. There are no sidewalks on the existing Fair Oaks Avenue Bridge.

At the Kifer Road/Fair Oaks Avenue interchange, north-south pedestrian movements are limited to the
east side of Fair Oaks Avenue and east-west crossing of Fair Oaks Avenue is limited to the north side of
Fair Oaks Avenue. Raised pedestrian refuge islands are located at the slip lanes on westbound Kifer Road
at North Fair Oaks Avenue, northbound north Fair Oaks Avenue at Kifer Road, northbound South Fair
Oaks Avenue at Evelyn Avenue and westbound Evelyn Avenue at South Fair Oaks Avenue. Pedestrians
crossing at these intersections must therefore cross in three stages.

BICYCLE FACILITIES

Bikeway planning and design in California typically relies on guidelines and design standards established
by California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) in the Highway Design Manual (Chapter 1000:
Bikeway Planning and Design). Caltrans provides for three distinct types of bikeway facilities, as described
below and shown on the accompanying figures.

    Class I Bikeway (Bike Path) provides a completely separate right-of-way and is designated for the
        exclusive use of bicycles and pedestrians with vehicle and pedestrian cross-flow minimized. In
        general, bike paths serve corridors not served by streets and highways or where sufficient right-
        of-way exists to allow such facilities to be constructed away from the influence of parallel streets
        and vehicle conflicts.




    Class II Bikeways (Bike Lanes) are lanes for bicyclists generally adjacent to the outer vehicle travel
        lanes. These lanes have special lane markings, pavement legends, and signage. Bicycle lanes are
        generally five (5) feet wide. Adjacent vehicle parking and vehicle/pedestrian cross-flow are
        permitted.




    Class III Bikeway (Bike Route) are designated by signs or pavement markings for shared use with
        pedestrians or motor vehicles, but have no separated bike right-of-way or lane striping. Bike

EXISTING TRANSIT SERVICE

Within the study area, VTA bus route 26 and 304 currently operate on Fair Oaks Avenue, VTA bus route 32
operates on Evelyn Avenue and VTA bus route 55 operates on Sunnyvale Avenue. The Fair Oaks Avenue
Bridge is located near the Sunnyvale Caltrain station. Figure 2 shows the existing transit service near the
project site, which are described in detail below and summarized in Table 2. The table includes the origin
and destination, the operating hours, the headways, and the average peak load factors. The average peak
load factor is a measure of resource utilization. It compares the supply of seats on a bus versus the
average peak number of on-board passengers aboard at any time during the peak period. For all-day
service, the average peak load factor is based on the average peak load factor over the entire day. For
reference a load factor of 1.0 indicates that all seats are full.

3. CONSTRUCTION CONDITIONS

Construction for the Fair Oaks Bridge Rehabilitation project is anticipated to occur over a one and a half-
year period. This section of the report addresses construction-related impacts of the project, specifically as
it relates to expected traffic impacts.


TRAFFIC PATTERNS

During construction, the Fair Oaks Avenue Bridge will provide at least one lane of traffic in each direction
for vehicle travel for the entire duration of construction. We do expect some traffic patterns to change to
avoid the construction area and traffic will likely re-route to Wolfe Road and Sunnyvale Avenue via Evelyn
Avenue and California Avenue. The existing traffic volumes and level of service were reviewed and since
the intersections at Evelyn Avenue and Kifer Road currently operate well we do not expect a substantial
shift. To provide a conservative analysis, we estimated that approximately 15% of traffic that travels over
the bridge will shift to an alternate route.

The resulting AM and PM peak hour traffic volumes and the intersection lane configurations during
construction is shown on Figure 5. Figure 6 displays the daily roadway segment volumes that are
expected due to the traffic pattern change.


TRAFFIC OPERATIONS

The AM and PM peak hour levels of service were calculated at the study intersection assuming
construction traffic volumes (with the traffic pattern change) and construction lane configuration.During
the entirety of construction, traffic on Fair Oaks Bridge will be limited to one vehicle lane and one bicycle
lane in each direction. During the first three construction stages, Hendy Avenue will be closed from Kifer
Road to the Home Depot driveway. During stage 4 of construction, Hendy Avenue will reopen, Evelyn
Avenue westbound traffic will be reduced from three to two lanes and Kifer Avenue at Fair Oaks Avenue
will be reduced from two lanes to one lane. The level of service analysis is based on the most restricted
lane configurations at the intersections. As shown in Table 4 the study intersections operate at an
acceptable LOS under construction conditions. The technical calculations are provided in Appendix C.
